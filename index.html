Who can use WRIO Internet OS, and why?

WRIO Internet OS was created to be an alternative to today’s Web 2.0 — cluttered, non-secure, and with large scale privacy breaches almost daily on the news. The purpose of this project is for everyone to benefit from a user-centric browsing experience utilizing the semantic web with automatic data processing and storing metadata on the large degree of security that a blockchain brings. Authors, Entrepreneurs, Developers, and Users will all be incentivized by WRIO’s user-centric services and DApps ecosystem.

What is the current state of the WRIO Internet OS project?

The MVP was launched in April 2018 and we’re on schedule (see Roadmap) to further develop the project to the point where we can soon bring a generation of fully distributed and secure Web 3.0 browsing.

How does WRIO Internet OS utilize the blockchain?

Blockchain technology will be utilized to store the metadata of each generated page (in Linked Data format) prior to being integrated to the user’s Interest Matrix compiled in their profile. It helps in the auto process of linking decentralized pages through a global metadatabase.

Why are you using Ethereum? 

Ethereum has matured dramatically over the past few years. Because of its maturity and therefore it’s blockchain security, as well as it’s scaling capability plans, we believe it’s the best option for us to run this platform on.

If you’re using Ethereum and storing metadata on it, won’t it bloat the network? 

The simple answer to that is no. Due to the semantic infrastructure of WRIO Internet OS, the metadata will equal ‘tags’ on the blockchain, and these tags will be limited to <64 bytes only, and every post on the platform, for instance, has a “wallet” attached to it with a list of transactions and metadata (tags). To put this in perspective, Wikipedia has about 5 million articles and the whole ETH transaction capacity is about .5 million per day. That means the whole wiki metadata equal to 10 days of ETH capacity only.

Can the team deliver on this large-scale promise?

In order for individuals to be accepted as team members, they must meet two key criteria: they must pursue lifelong learning as a basic principle, and have a strong drive towards creating a better world [alternative]. How will WGD price be maintained after the crowdsale and beyond?

WGD’s price will be maintained by webRunes’ Buyback Fund, which will ensure the price of WGD does not go below the price of sale in the initial public offering. Users will be able to sell WGD at any time after the Crowdsale on the book price, which directly depends on the number of collected ETH and issued WGD, which provides a stable exchange rate. (see webGold Emission)

What cryptocurrency can I use to contribute in the crowdsale?

We are only accepting ETH in our crowdsale. Please do not send any other token/cryptocurrency.

What’s the difference between WGD, CRD, and WRIO OS tokens?

WGD (web‘Runes’Gold) will be traded as a stake in the platform and will be traded on exchanges once the Crowdsale is concluded. CRD (Credits) are in-platform tokens to be used as Credit inside the platform, such as upvotes to authors, donations to users, or content upvotes for a better user experience (see Interest Matrix algorithm). And WRIO OS tokens will be shares of the platform.

What happens if not all tokens are sold?

Unsold tokens will be burned. Please see more details in the White Paper.
